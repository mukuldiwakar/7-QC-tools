Check Sheet

Definition

A structured form or table used to systematically collect and record data in real-time at the location where the data is generated.

Significance

  • Helps in collecting consistent data quickly.
  • Makes data gathering visual and understandable.
  • Identifies patterns in occurrence of issues.

Use in BPO

  • Used to track how many times AHT (Average Handling Time) exceeds the target in a day.
  • Record the number of errors in different categories (wrong tag, delayed response, escalation).
  • Example: A QA might use it to tally how many quality violations happened in a shift.

Control Chart

Definition

A line graph with upper and lower control limits (UCL, LCL) that displays the performance of a process over time to see if it is under control or fluctuating.

Significance

  • Distinguishes between common (normal) and special (abnormal) variations.
  • Allows proactive intervention before performance drops.
  • Ensures process stability over time.

Use in BPO

  • Track AHT, CSAT (Customer Satisfaction), FCR (First Call Resolution) daily.
  • Check if your team’s AHT stays within set limits across weeks.
  • Example: If AHT spikes above UCL, it signals an issue needing attention.

Cause and Effect Diagram (Fishbone/Ishikawa)

Definition

A visual tool used to identify, explore, and display possible causes of a specific problem in a structured way.

Significance

  • Finds root causes instead of symptoms.
  • Encourages brainstorming in teams.
  • Helps eliminate guesswork.

Use in BPO

  • Used to analyze why CSAT is dropping.
  • Breaks down causes into categories: People, Process, Tools, Environment.
  • Example: High escalation rate may be caused by poor product knowledge (People) or system lag (Tools).

Histogram

Definition

A bar chart representing the frequency distribution of numerical data, grouped into ranges.

Significance

  • Shows distribution, variation, and central tendency.
  • Easily detects skewness and abnormalities in data.
  • Aids in decision-making by visual evidence.

Use in BPO

  • Visualize how many chats fell in different AHT time ranges.
  • Example: 20 chats took 5–7 minutes, 50 chats took 8–10 minutes. This shows where most of the workload lies.

Pareto Chart

Definition

A bar chart sorted in descending order with a cumulative percentage line, based on the Pareto Principle (80/20 rule).

Significance

  • Identifies the few vital causes that cause the most problems.
  • Helps prioritize issues for corrective actions.
  • Drives efficient resource allocation.

Use in BPO

  • Identify top reasons for customer dissatisfaction or escalations.
  • Example: If 80% of complaints come from just 3 out of 10 possible reasons, solving those 3 has the highest impact.

Scatter Diagram

Definition

A graph where two variables are plotted along two axes to determine their correlation or relationship.

Significance

  • Shows whether a relationship exists between two factors.
  • Helps in root cause analysis through correlation.
  • Identifies trends and patterns visually.

Use in BPO

  • Analyze if there’s a connection between training hours and error rates.
  • Example: If agents with more training have fewer escalations, it shows a negative correlation.

Stratification (aka Flow Chart or Run Chart)

Definition

A technique for separating data from different sources or categories to identify patterns, relationships, or root causes more clearly.

Significance

  • Breaks down complex data into meaningful layers.
  • Reveals differences across teams, shifts, or locations.
  • Prevents misleading conclusions from combined data.

Use in BPO

  • Compare AHT during morning vs night shifts.
  • Analyze CSAT for different product categories or support teams.
  • Example: High error rate may only occur in one shift—this wouldn’t be seen in combined data.
